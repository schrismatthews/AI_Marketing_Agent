# Content

**What this is:** the content side of marketing, the videos, posts, and podcasts you put out at the very top of the funnel. Content is the front door to your business, and this note is about building a door people actually want to walk through. (For the gated version of content, see the lead magnets note. For where content ideas come from and how to reuse it in email, see the email note. For running content as a video ad and the pixel it builds, see the Facebook ads note. For the funnel it feeds, see the fundamentals. For the actual writing, see copywriting.)

## Content is the front door

The type of content you put out can make or break you. It's the entrance point of the whole funnel, the first thing anyone ever sees of you. This is your door. If the door looks run down and crappy, nobody comes inside, no matter how good the rest of the building is. So the content has to be good, because everything downstream depends on someone walking through it first.

## Content is just an ungated lead magnet

Here's the simplest way to think about it. Content is a lead magnet with no opt-in on the front. If you're trying to come up with a piece of content, come up with a lead magnet idea, then just don't gate it. Make it the video, the post, the podcast episode, and give it away. Same DNA, no email required.

And it does not have to be video. A blog post is content. A podcast episode is content. A video is content. Anything that helps somebody is content. I like using video, because Facebook's native pixel tags everyone who watches, so the content does double duty: it helps people AND it builds your retargeting pool at the same time (more on that in the ads note). But video is a preference, not a requirement.

One more thing that makes content worth the effort: the same piece does two jobs. The content you make for the top of your funnel is also the content you send in your welcome campaign and your regular emails. You make it once and you use it twice. And you probably already have a pile of it. We've got a podcast and a blog with 12 years of stuff in it, so a lot of the time we just pull the best of what already exists and use that. (Where to dig up content ideas is covered in the email note.)

## The same rule as everything: help them, don't talk about yourself

Content follows the exact same rule as your copy and your ads: be cool, help people, solve a problem. If your content is about your company, how long you've been in business, and the services you offer, it is not going to work. You might scrape one or two leads out of it, but that's it. Content has to be about the customer and their problem, helping them before you ever ask for anything.

So you make something genuinely helpful. It solves a real problem, or it hands them a resource or an angle they didn't have before. After they watch it, they feel good about you, and that good feeling is what makes the next step, opting into your lead magnet, easy. You earned it by helping first.

## Work backwards from the funnel

Once your funnel actually exists (you can't do this before it does), the easiest way to know what content to make is to work backwards. Start at your profit maximizer, then your core offer, then your tripwire, then your lead magnet. Once you can see the whole chain, the content writes itself, because you know exactly what it's feeding into.

I won't pretend the hard part isn't real: how do you give away good content for free and still have something left to put behind the lead magnet? It can be tough. Two ways through it. One, dissect the problem into parts, and let the content solve one part while the lead magnet handles the next. Two, skip the lead magnet entirely on that piece and send people straight to a tripwire. Sometimes that works just fine.

## Content and the lead magnet: same person, not the same topic

Your lead magnet does not have to be on the same topic as your content. They just have to serve the same person. The content is what identifies who's paying attention.

Say you're a dog trainer and you make a video on how to get a new puppy to stop peeing in the house. Notice you just targeted one specific sect, new puppy owners, with one specific problem. Now think about what you know about anyone who watched it: they own a new puppy and they want the peeing to stop. So your lead magnet doesn't have to be about house-training at all. It just has to answer the next question that same person has: what else does a new puppy owner need to know? The content reveals who they are. The lead magnet escalates for that same person.

## Always point to the next step

Every piece of content needs a next step, every time. Content points to the lead magnet, the lead magnet points to the tripwire, and you're always moving them up to the next thing. A piece of content with no next step is a dead end, and a dead end is attention you got and then wasted.

So put the link everywhere it fits. At the end of a content video: "by the way, I'm giving away [the thing], click the link below to grab it." Under the post. As a pre-roll or mid-roll ad inside your podcast. Wherever the content lives, the path to the next step lives with it.

## How to measure it

Here's a principle that runs through the whole funnel: each stage has one primary goal, and the next step is a welcome byproduct, not the scorecard.

For content, the primary goal is the view, not the sale and not even the opt-in. You're not making money directly off this content yet, so the win is getting cheap views. A good video with the right targeting should not be overpaying for attention, so your main number is cost per view. The lower you can get it, the better.

If you've attached a lead magnet to a content ad, then you watch two numbers: cost per view and cost per opt-in. But keep your head straight about it: the opt-in is a byproduct, not the goal. It's the same way a lead-magnet ad's real goal is getting them on the list, and a tripwire sale right after is just gravy. Judge each piece on its actual job. (The deeper measurement lives in the analytics note.)

## The bottom line

Content is the door to everything else you do. Make it genuinely helpful, aim it at one specific person, always point it at the next step, and keep your cost per view low. Do that and the door keeps bringing the right people in cheaply, and the rest of the funnel does the selling.
