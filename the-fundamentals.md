# The Marketing Funnel Fundamentals

*Authored by Jaredrhod*

I started my business in 2014, and by the end of 2015, we had **grossed over one million dollars.** It has steadily grown ever since, and it’s all because of the talent, the marketing, and the funnels.

I’m going to share with you **the process that we use** to generate this income.

Most people charge a lot of money to teach the stuff. I’m sharing it with you because I have enough money, and I don’t want your money.

**Plus it’s my way to help and give back.**

You’ll learn how to build a sales funnel that will automate your business, increase revenue, and give you freedom. If you follow the process, and take the time to implement these steps, **I’m convinced it will change your life.**

So buckle up, and let’s get started.

#### **First, It’s Not About You**

**Instead of focusing on what you do, focus on what people desire.**

For example, if you sell makeup, your customer wants to look good. If you’re a painter, your customer wants a fresh, new look for their home that will make it more enjoyable for them when they walk into a room.

If you’re a tree trimmer, your customer probably wants to ensure a tree isn’t going to fall on their home…or they are tired of picking up sticks in their yard.

The most important aspect to remember about marketing is that it’s not about you. It’s not even about your product.

**It’s about the end result and how customers feel when they get it.**

Most (not all) people buy on emotions, not logic. Ask yourself these questions: How will they feel? How will their life change?

I’d advise that you make a list of your answers.

**It’s not about solving the problem, it’s about the change of state in the customer after the problem is solved.**

I sell online horse training, and when I write my sales copy, I focus on what I know our customers want to feel due to whatever they may be buying from us.

I know they want…

- To be able to get on their horse and enjoy the ride
- To avoid dealing with problematic behaviors
- To have confidence in themselves and their abilities
- To have a good relationship with their horse
- To be able to trust their horse that he won’t hurt them

There are more, but those are pretty standard across the board for us, no matter what we’re selling in the horse niche.

Once you’ve made a list, and determined the desired outcomes for your customers or clients, you’re ready to start your first sales funnel.

#### **The Sales Funnel**

The sales process looks like this:

**Content -> Lead Magnet -> Tripwire -> Core Offer -> Profit Maximizer**

I will break each of these down in detail and do my best to give great examples of how to apply them to your business.

One thing about building this: the funnel runs in the order above, but you plan it in reverse. Start with the last thing that happens to a customer and work backwards, because every earlier step only exists to feed the next one. That said, you do not need the whole funnel built before you start. You can begin making content and dropping your pixel on day one, before you have a single lead magnet or offer, because that content is just building awareness and is not sending anyone anywhere yet. Build your audience while you build the back half of the funnel. Do not let "I have to set all of this up first" stop you from starting today.

The idea behind a sales funnel is to introduce people to you/your product and move the relationship forward.

Ryan Deiss relates marketing to human relationships.

> **You’d first introduce yourself (content), then maybe go out for coffee (lead magnet), then go on a date (tripwire), then start a relationship (core offer), and finally you’d get married (profit maximizer).**

This is the reason sales funnels work so well.

Instead of running ads and immediately asking people to buy your stuff, you establish goodwill by creating a relationship with your leads and customers.

Trust and relationships also create **repeat** customers.

#### **The Content (Introduce Yourself)**

What are the questions your customers ask you the most? What is most misunderstood in your niche? What does everyone in your niche need to understand before making a purchase?

Once you’ve answered these questions, create some content answering these questions. Your content could be in the form of a podcast, blog post, or video and then publish it to channels like Facebook, Instagram, Tiktok, YouTube, Wordpress, wherever.

**Here are some examples of content that could be created from different niches:**

- A roofer makes a video explaining the signs of a roof that needs to be replaced
- A real estate agent explains the importance of having a virtual tour
- A hairstylist shows how to do a unique and easy hairstyle
- A tree trimmer makes a video explaining the difference between a healthy and unhealthy tree
- A yard maintenance man demonstrates how to water your grass properly
- Someone who sells makeup makes a video on how to apply a good foundation
- Someone who sells paintings makes a video showing off their skills
- Someone who sells pies writes a recipe for the perfect key lime pie

Once you’ve come up with some ideas for content, it’s time to start putting it out in the world.

Depending on where you're advertising, you want to make sure that you're pixeling your traffic. This can be through Google Analytics, the Facebook pixel, and many others. The reason you want to set this up is so that you can re-target people who consume your content.

If you're going to advertise through Facebook, make sure you create your pixel in your Facebook business account. If you don't have a business account, and you're planning to advertise on Facebook, do that first.

AI can help you set all of this up if you're uncomfortable or unfamiliar with setting up pixels.

Now, once it's time to start getting your content out in the world, begin by selecting your audience.

For example, if you’re a local hairstylist, you would ideally target women who live within a certain radius of your location. You could also narrow your targeting by specifying an age and income level range.

Facebook, for example, has many different ad objectives to choose from and your medium (video, blog article, podcast) will determine what you should use.

If you want to go to the extra effort, you can create different ad sets and target groups of people individually to determine which group yields the best results.

If you're using Facebook to distribute this top-of-funnel content, the stuff whose only job is to build awareness before you ask anyone for anything, use "awareness" as the campaign objective. You're not trying to make a sale at this stage, so you don't want a conversion objective yet.

With that said, there is a way to distribute your content and build a following. You can do this with organic, non-paid marketing. Literally $0 spent. But here's what you need to remember when you're creating your content, whether paid or organic:

**The goal of creating content is not to make sales…yet.** It’s to *raise awareness* of your brand and help people.

Now, the question I get asked most is, "Yeah but if I tell them how to do the thing then they won't buy from me".

And that's where you're wrong.

Frank Kern is someone who I learned a lot from and he always said he gives away everything free, even his best stuff.

Why? It creates goodwill and goodwill create future sales.

Be cool, actually help people, give away your best content, and you'll become the most followed person in your niche.

Because, here's the thing...

If you have a following and/or an email list of people who already like you and consume your content, it's going to be very easy to sell to them. And if you have a very large following of those same types of people, it's only going to be that much easier.

A big following who actually like you is how you start, and it makes selling easy. But the thing you ultimately want is a list, email or text, of those same people. A following lives on someone else's platform and can vanish overnight. A list is yours, and nobody can take it. The following is the head start. The owned list is how you win.

Now...

Once you start creating that following and getting people pixeled, it's time to start owning that audience. You can do this with text or email, although email is a lot simpler to set up and text marketing is heavily regulated.

Now, each platform has different ways to reach people who have consumed your content and teaching you the technical side of how to do that is not what this is. You can ask AI to help you with all the technical stuff.

For example, you can run re-targeting ads on Facebook or tiktok.

Or, if you have that large following, you could just make a new piece of content and then slide in the lead magnet.

#### **The Lead Magnet (Coffee)**

"A Lead Magnet is an irresistible bribe that gives a specific chunk of value to a prospect in exchange for their contact information." This is a direct quote from Ryan Deiss, a God of marketing.

Ideally they can consume it fast, but that is a guideline, not a law. One of my best lead magnets ever is a 30-Day Action Plan, and it takes 30 days to actually work through. The real bar is this: it gets delivered the second they opt in, it actually gives the answer to the problem, and it makes them glad they handed over their email. Fast to consume helps. Real value wins.

**A lead magnet could be a…**

- video
- checklist
- report
- cheat sheet
- resource list
- template
- discount
- quiz

It needs to be **specific** and answer one big question.

The main difference between a lead magnet vs. content is that people now need to give you their email address (or phone number) to get the thing you're offering them.

And here's the line between a lead magnet and a tripwire, because people mix them up: it comes down to what the prospect gives up. If all they give you is an email, it's a lead magnet. If they have to give up their time (sitting through an hour-long webinar) or access to their wallet (a free trial that asks for a credit card), that's really a tripwire, even if you never charge them. Keep them straight.

**Examples of BAD lead magnets**

- Sign up for our newsletter (not specific)
- Learn how to do marketing (too generic)
- Get our updates and special offers (names a delivery mechanism, not an outcome)

**Examples of GOOD lead magnets**

Example from my own business:

If You Knew Your Horse Could Handle Whatever Happens, Would You Still Be Nervous To Ride?

Enter your email below and I'll give you my free Confidence Checklist. It finds every missing gap in your horse's understanding. That's where your confidence went, and it's how you get it back.

This headline converts at over 50% to cold traffic.

Why does it work?

It's specific and solves one problem: The customer wants to be confident when they ride.

Here's another one:

How to make sure you pick a good wedding photographer. 

Download my checklist where I'll reveal 10 specific qualities that determines what makes a good photographer.

Plus you'll learn all the red flags to avoid to ensure you don't pick the wrong one.

Ultra specific and solves one problem, right? That's a good lead magnet.

A good way to determine whether or not your lead magnet is relevant to your audience, and something they want, is to **look at your opt-in rate**.

For example, if 100 people visit your squeeze page where they can sign up for your lead magnet, and 35 of them submit their email addresses, then you’d have a 35% opt-in rate (which is pretty standard).

**Below 25% needs work, 25 to 35% is solid, and above 35% is great. Better than 50% is exceptional, and it's reachable on a narrow, specific offer to the right traffic, like my Confidence Checklist, which clears 50% to cold.**

#### **Setting Up Your Lead Magnet**

A lead magnet performs best on a *squeeze page*.

A **squeeze page** is a page where the website visitor only has two options.

They can opt-in (submit their email) for your lead magnet or leave the page. That’s it.

There are no other links for them to click and nothing else on the page for them to do other than opt-in.

You can *set up a squeeze page on your existing website or use a dedicated funnel builder*

There are lots of options out there but my two favorites are LeadPages (integrates directly with AI through an MCP server) or Instapage (dead simple drag and drop page builder).

To collect their email address and send them the thing they signed up for (the lead magnet), **you’ll need some type of email autoresponder**.

Some website builders have built-in autoresponders, but I **highly recommend ActiveCampaign.**

I’ve used almost every autoresponder out there, and I can confidently tell you that ActiveCampaign is the best one.

Most website builders have a native integration with ActiveCampaign and will allow you to connect your website form to your ActiveCampaign account via API.

One thing to note: every disconnect in a funnel costs you customers. When a prospect moves from one step to the next, the next step must acknowledge what they just did, or you create a "wait, what just happened?" moment, and that moment is where they bounce. For example, imagine you have a lead magnet and then when the user opts-in for the lead magnet and then nothing happens or worse they land on a non-relevant page.

You have to make sure that the page the prospect lands on directly after they opt-in acknowledges that their lead magnet is on the way to their inbox (or wherever you sent it).

Also, at this point in your funnels, your prospect is excited, so it's a great time to make them a tripwire offer (more on tripwire offers below).

So, at the top of your page (the one they land on after the opt in), put a short acknowledgment line: "Thanks, your [thing] is on its way in X minutes, and there's something you should know..." The trailing ellipsis pulls them straight into the headline of the tripwire.

**How To Deliver The Lead Magnet**

Once you’ve got your autoresponder connected to your web form, you’ll want to **set up an automatic email that delivers the lead magnet** to the person who opted in for it.

In this email, you’ll also want to make them aware of your tripwire offer (if you have one).

So, this email does two things.

1. It delivers the thing they signed up for (what I like to do is store my deliverables on Google Drive or Amazon AWS and then provide the direct link to the file in the email)
2. It pitches them your tripwire (optional)

Now, you should expect your tripwire to have a low conversion rate at this point. After all, these are brand new people who just signed up for your list and you're still at the "coffee" stage.

However, once they become a lead and you've met for coffee, you can ask your prospect out on an actual date.

#### **The Tripwire (Your First “Official” Date)**

A tripwire is a splinter of your core offer (the main thing you sell).

It’s a **piece you can pull from your product and sell for dirt cheap** to convert leads (people who opted into your lead magnet) to customers.

The *only* purpose of a tripwire is to get your leads to open their wallets and buy from you.

It’s an irresistible offer that they simply can’t say no to.

**Here are some examples of great tripwires:**

- A guitar company sells 50 guitar picks for $5
- One section of an online course for $7
- McDonald’s sells a hamburger for $.99
- A makeup company sells a sample kit for $9.99
- Godaddy offers your first domain registration for $1.00

**Service-based businesses** will often give a steep discount on services to get new customers in the door.

Also, it’s important to note that a **tripwire’s primary purpose is to break even** (or even lose some money on the front end).

You are not trying to make a profit here.

You’re trying to get people to open their wallets and buy from you.

When someone opens their wallet for the first time, even if it’s just $1, they are far more likely to open it repeatedly.

A **financial transaction moves the relationship** between you and your customer to the next step.

You’ve introduced yourself (content), bought them coffee (lead magnet), and now you’re going on an actual date.

I hope you can begin to see why it’s called a funnel.

You can now email these prospects and ask them to buy your tripwire.

Additionally, you should now create a custom audience on the platform you're advertising on and start running ads with an offer to buy your tripwire.

#### **The Core Offer (An Official Relationship)**

This is the main thing you sell.

If you have a business, then you have a core offer.

**The best time to pitch the core offer is immediately after your customer purchased your tripwire**.

They are in the right mindset and are more likely to make a second transaction.

**One-click upsells are ideal** because customers don’t have to enter their contact and payment information again.

The more barriers you can remove, the more likely they will buy.

And since they just bought a portion (splinter) of your product via the tripwire, it would make sense that some will want the complete package.

Additionally, **you should pitch your core offer in the thank you email** sent to those who purchased your tripwire.

It’s just another opportunity to buy.

And just like every other step, you can **run retargeting ads** to people who purchased your tripwire but did not purchase your core offer.

Your prospects are now in the "dating" phase of the funnel and you can send them emails with various offers for your core product.

#### **The Profit Maximizer (Marriage)**

This is something that is a supplement to your core offer but offers tremendous value for both you and your customer.

**Some businesses don’t have a profit maximizer, and if yours doesn’t, you are missing out on a ton of potential revenue.**

Examples of profit maximizers:

- An monthly service subscription for an AC company
- A PRO tier of a membership website
- A pressure washing add-on for a painting company

Just like before, you want to ideally offer your profit maximizer directly after the core offer, whether that's through email or an upsell directly after they purchase.

#### **Conclusion**

Marketing is a lot like that famous line in Pirates of the Carribean. These are more like guidelines than actual rules. Sometimes you may break the rules. For example, sometimes I run tripwire ads on Facebook to cold traffic and it converts. I completely skip the lead magnet phase. It's all about testing and finding out what works best for your business. However, what I've shared with you here are the core principles of marketing and it's my baseline for everything I do. Follow it but don't be afraid to break the rules if it makes sense.
